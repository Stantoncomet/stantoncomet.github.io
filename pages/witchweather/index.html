    Resurgence of Witch Hunting

    A artistic depiction of a 16th century farm

    This happened between the 13th-18th centuries.

    One possible reason was the changing climate. It was getting colder that normal in lots of places.

    "Little Ice Age"

    "The Little Ice Age was brutal." -National Geographic

    This led to a lot of people with less than satisfactory crop yields, which made them unhappy.

    Hence, the "enlightened" people of renaissance Europe held trials for witches as scapegoats for all their problems.

    Multicountry Data

    General trends of temperature x trials

    Measuring temperature and frequency of witch trials in 11 different European countries shows some correlation.

    Specfically, there appeared to be an influx of witch trials during lower temperatures.

    • Note: data are "standardized", and based on deviations from mean values for each region.
    • Concerns: natural opposite directional trends relating to time
    • And: critical events in history that can change data results

    Another Data Trend

    The balancing act of trying to keep a bunch of humans alive

    Since crop yield is our primary interest and supposed reason for the witch trials here, we can also look at economic growth trends.

    Something something "proxies"... We can measure economic growth by meauring two other things.

    • # of cities in a country (urbanization)
    • population density of cities

    Another Data Trend

    Economic growth by population density

    "Areas with slower-than-average population growth had more witch trials; areas with faster-than-average population growth had fewer witch trials (223)."

    Another trend, further supporting the idea of increased concern for witches during troubling times.
